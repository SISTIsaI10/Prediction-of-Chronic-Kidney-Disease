Chronic kidney disease

CKD is a progressive condition that affects the kidneys' ability to function properly, leading to a buildup of waste in the body. Early detection and proactive management are key to living a healthier life with CKD.

Symptoms and Diagnosis

How to recognise chronic kidney disease early

High Blood pressure

Chronic high blood pressure puts strain on the small blood vessels in the kidneys, leading to gradual damage. Over time, this damage can result in CKD..

Diabetes

Uncontrolled diabetes is a leading cause of CKD. Elevated blood sugar levels over time can damage the blood vessels in the kidneys, impacting their ability to function properly..

Glomerulonephritis

This term refers to inflammation of the kidney's filtering units (glomeruli). Infections, autoimmune diseases, or other conditions can cause glomerulonephritis, contributing to the development of CKD.
